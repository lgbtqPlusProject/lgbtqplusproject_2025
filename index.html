Our Mission

The LGBTQ+ Project is a digital archive committed to preserving and celebrating LGBTQ+ history. Through storytelling, photographs, artifacts, and community voices, we document the past to empower the future.

From iconic protests to personal moments of courage, this site uplifts our shared legacy and ensures it remains accessible to all generations. We honor the voices that came before us — and those still being written.

✊🏽 Honoring Bayard Rustin

Bayard Rustin was a master strategist of nonviolent resistance and a key architect of the civil rights movement. As a gay Black man, Rustin faced both racism and homophobia while leading with courage and grace behind the scenes.

From organizing the 1963 March on Washington to mentoring Dr. Martin Luther King Jr., Rustin’s contributions to justice are immeasurable—and yet often overlooked.

🕊️ Rustin’s Legacy: His identity didn’t weaken his activism—it shaped it. His life reminds us that being true to who we are can be our greatest strength in the fight for equity.

📅 Bayard Rustin: A Life in Moments

1947 – Journey of Reconciliation

Bayard Rustin co-organized the first Freedom Ride to challenge segregation on interstate buses, paving the way for the 1960s Freedom Rides.

1963 – March on Washington

Rustin was the chief organizer of the historic march where Dr. King delivered his “I Have a Dream” speech, uniting over 250,000 people.

1980s – LGBTQ+ Advocacy

In his later years, Rustin spoke out as a proud gay man, advocating for LGBTQ+ rights and aligning queer liberation with civil rights.
